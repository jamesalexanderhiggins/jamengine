FAQ

Questions we hear a lot.

```
Do I really own my platform, or is it more like renting?+
You truly own it. Your platform is deployed on your own free accounts (Vercel, Supabase, Cloudinary). Jam Engine sets it up and hands over full access. If Jam Engine ceased to exist tomorrow, your site would keep running because it lives on infrastructure you control. We charge once to build it, then we step back.
What does "zero ongoing fees" actually mean?+
The accounts that run your platform (Vercel, Supabase, Cloudinary) all have generous free tiers that cover everything a working musician needs. Stripe charges their standard processing fee on every tip — typically around 2–3% — but that goes to Stripe, not to Jam Engine. We take nothing from your tips, ever.
What if a fan pays a tip and I don't play their song?+
This is something every platform has to address. By design, a tip-to-request is a request, not a guarantee — that's stated clearly in the fan-facing interface. You're in control of your setlist and never obligated to play anything. That said, the platform is designed to make it easy to honour requests, and the queue system makes it clear what fans have asked for.
I'm not technical at all. Can I still use this?+
Absolutely. The entire setup process is handled by our team using the information you provide in your onboarding form. You'll create a few free accounts (we guide you through each one with step-by-step instructions), and then we do the rest. Your artist portal has a setup wizard for any configuration you need to do yourself — it's designed for non-technical users and includes a skip button for anything you already know.
Can I upgrade from Starter to Complete later?+
Yes. You'll pay the difference ($201) at any time and we'll upgrade your instance. You don't lose any of your song database, show history, or fan data in the process.
I already use Songbook Pro. Can I import my songs?+
Yes. Jam Engine Complete includes a Songbook Pro importer. Export your songs from Songbook Pro as an .sbp file and import it into your Jam Engine portal — your entire catalog transfers including titles, artists, chord charts, and default keys. You can also paste from Ultimate Guitar or enter songs manually.
Does this work for online streams and YouTube shows?+
Yes. During show setup, paste your YouTube Live, Twitch, or Facebook Live URL. Your stream embed appears on the public hub and fan view. Online fans join the same live queue as in-room fans and interact identically. You can run a full hybrid show — in-venue audience and online audience in the same experience simultaneously.
What about chat moderation? What if someone is abusive?+
Jam Engine has a two-tier block system. A soft block raises the minimum tip a user needs to have their messages appear. A hard block silences them entirely — but still accepts their tips. You can apply either type for the current show only or permanently. A keyword filter catches flagged content automatically and shows it with a red indicator on your screen before it appears publicly.
```
